Trip to Europe
The number of times I have typed and started over trying to write out this experience is laughable. Maybe another time I will provide a detailed report or blog post about my trip and how it all came to be but trying to summarize it while keeping it all relevant is challenging. I have the utmost respect for all writers out there. Now, on we travel to Europe.

I took two trips to Hungary, the first lasted about a week, and the second lasted nearly two months. Although this was a trip to Europe, the main reasoning behind these trips was that my girlfriend at the time lived in Hungary although we worked together in Georgia prior to her moving back to Europe. This first visit to Hungary; we visited Budapest, and it is a beautiful city. The history behind the name of the city was something that stood out to me more so than all of the sights I saw. Budapest was not always “Budapest” as we know, the city itself is divided in half by the Danube River and eastern part of the city is known as Pest whereas the western part is called Buda. Hungary has centuries of history, and it wasn’t until the 1870’s that the two towns unified becoming Budapest. Now I do have a gripe with this city as the few occasions we took a train to the city, it was cold and rainy each time. It didn’t make for the best conditions despite the sun and clear skies in the photos above. That was the only day out of seven or eight days of us being there that it was sunny. Another interesting point is that the city itself has an older feel to it. If you were to picture an American city and think if that is what the capital of a European country would look like, then you’re wrong. There aren’t large skyscrapers or modern 20-floor buildings. Maybe in another country like England or Germany? I am guessing here, but at least for Budapest, the city itself does not stray from its history.

As for the last photo, that picture was taken at Rab Island which is located in the Adriatic Sea off the coast of Croatia. This was the clearest water I have ever experienced. I could look down while swimming and see the seafloor 20-feet under me clearly! I’m used to being at the beaches here in North Carolina and South Carolina in murky green water and wondering “what just touched my foot?”. I know I didn’t give out too much detail about the whole experience, but I am struggling to not write paragraphs on end about this. One thing I wanted to try and say using this as an example is that if you have the chance to do something memorable and exciting, do it. I am 26, it is now 2023, and I earned my AS back in 2017. I regret a lot of decision I made in between then and now as I feel I have wasted so much time of my life, but not once have I ever regretted the decisions that led me to visiting Europe.
